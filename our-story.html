It Started with Bedtime Stories

I'm Nathan, 36, from Scotland. I have two daughters—one is 4, the other just turned 1.

When my eldest was 2, bedtime became... challenging. She wanted stories. New stories. Different stories. Stories about princesses, dragons, robots, whatever popped into her head that day.

I'm not a natural storyteller. I'd fumble through half-remembered fairy tales. The Three Little Pigs, The Boy Who Cried Wolf—I knew them, but the details would escape me at 8 PM after a long day. Reciting them in an engaging way? Not my strong suit.

So I did what any exhausted parent in 2023 would do: I turned to ChatGPT.

There Should Be an App for This

ChatGPT worked brilliantly for stories. But it wasn't designed for bedtime. I had to:

• Type prompts on my phone in the dark
• Copy and paste stories
• Lost all our previous stories (my daughter would ask "Tell me about that bunny story again!")
• No way to save characters she loved
• Couldn't find good versions of classic fairy tales when she asked for them

I searched for an app that did what I needed. There were a few AI story apps, but nothing that felt right—nothing that remembered our stories, let me create characters, or had a library of classics in one place.

I thought: "I could build this. How hard could it be?"

Spoiler: Very hard.

My daughter called them "fairy stories," so FairyAI was born.

Eight Months of Building It Right

I thought it would take a couple of weeks. Eight months later, here we are.

Why so long? Because I wanted it done properly:

• Safe for children (COPPA compliant, no data tracking)
• Secure payment systems that actually work
• Multiple AI providers so it wouldn't break
• A library of 100+ free stories (because parents asked for them)
• Characters that kids could use in different stories
• Songs, narrations, multiple languages

Every time I fixed something, something else would break. Test, break, fix. Fix one thing, break another. There were days I questioned if it was worth continuing.

What Kept Me Going

Honestly? If I'd known it would take 8 months and hundreds of hours, I might have reconsidered.

But three things kept me pushing through:

First, my daughter's reaction. Watching her face light up when we brought her story ideas to life—a brave bunny, a robot who loved art, whatever random thing she thought of that day. That made every broken feature worth fixing.

Second, validation from other parents. Friends tested it with their kids—ages 2 through 12—and they actually used it. They came back with feedback, asked for features, told me their kids were asking for "the story app."

Third, I'd invested too much to quit. But more than that, I could see it coming together. I was watching something I built actually work and matter to real families.

Built for My Children, Not for Profit

Let me be clear about what FairyAI is and isn't:

What it is:

• An app I built for my daughter and families like ours
• A helping hand when you're exhausted and need a story
• A tool that complements books, not replaces them
• Something that will keep growing based on your feedback

What it isn't:

• Disney—it won't be perfect
• A get-rich-quick scheme—I built this because I needed it
• Just another AI app—this is specifically designed for bedtime and story time

I have plans to grow it: partnerships with real book companies, learning features, content for younger babies (sounds and simple stories), and more. This is just step 1... if it takes off.
