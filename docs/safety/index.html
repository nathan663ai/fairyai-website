Safety isn't a feature. It's the foundation.

You're trusting us with bedtime. Here's exactly what we do (and don't do) to earn that trust.

What We Never Do

These aren't just policies. They're hard lines we won't cross.

✗No ads or trackers

We make money from subscriptions, not advertising. No analytics that follow your child, no third-party tracking pixels.

✗No selling data to third parties

Your family's data stays with us. We never sell, rent, or share personal information with advertisers or data brokers.

✗No storing unnecessary personal information

We only keep what's needed to run the service: email, password (hashed), subscription status. No addresses, no phone numbers, no detailed profiles.

✗We never require photos of your child

Characters are designed to be created from imagination, traits and parent-approved images. You control what goes in.

What We Actively Enforce

Technical safeguards running 24/7 to keep FairyAI safe and stable.

✓Age-based content filters

Every story passes through filters that block violence, fear, explicit content, and substances, tailored to each age group (2-3, 4-6, 7-9, 10-12, 12+).

✓Age-appropriate word counts and themes

Toddlers get 400-word stories with comfort themes. Teens get 2000-word stories with complex morals. Vocabulary and concepts adapt automatically.

✓Rate limiting and abuse protection

Per-user, per-IP, per-operation limits prevent abuse. You cannot accidentally rack up huge bills or flood the system.

✓Input length limits and file size restrictions

Text inputs are capped. File uploads have a 16MB limit. These prevent malicious use and keep the service stable for everyone.

✓Secure passwords and browser protection

Passwords are encrypted (never stored in plain text). Every page uses browser security features that block malicious scripts and force secure connections.

✓Soft deletion with recovery window

When you delete your account, we keep it for 30 days in case you change your mind. After that, it's permanently removed.

✓No PII in logs

Our error logging doesn't capture personally identifiable information, IP addresses, or sensitive request bodies.
